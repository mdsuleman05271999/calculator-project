Compound Interest Calculator




ADD

A compound interest calculator is a tool used to determine the interest earned on an investment over a period of time, taking into account the effect of compound interest. Compound interest is the interest earned on both the original principal and the accumulated interest over time.

The formula for calculating compound interest is:

Formula: A = P * (1 + r/n)^(nt)

Where:

A = the final amount of the investment, including both the original principal and the interest earned

P = the initial principal (the original amount invested)

r = the annual interest rate

n = the number of times the interest is compounded in a year

t = the number of years the investment is held

Using a compound interest calculator, you can input the initial principal, annual interest rate, the number of times the interest is compounded, and the number of years the investment is held, to determine the final amount of the investment.

Compound interest calculators can be found online or as a feature in many financial software programs. They provide a convenient and easy way to calculate the potential return on an investment, and can be used to compare different investment options and to plan for long-term financial goals, such as saving for retirement.
